# Mini Aquatic Ecosystems – Experiment 01

## Objective
To observe how different biological inputs affect small aquatic systems.

## Hypothesis
Systems C and D are expected to show earlier biological activity 
due to higher initial microbial load. System B will show gradual 
changes from decomposition. System A should remain unchanged.

## Sample Origin
San Miguel, Cañas, Guanacaste, Costa Rica.
Samples collected from thermal springs and creek.

## Systems
A – Control  
B – Leaf (organic input)  
C – Biofilm (microbial input)  
D – Thermal water  

## Setup
- Plastic containers
- Partial gas exchange (covered with perforated film)
- Indirect light conditions

## Preliminary Observations
By Day 4, a white/translucent surface film appeared in all systems, consistent with microbial biofilm formation at the air-water interface. Systems C and D show greater surface coverage than A and B, supporting the hypothesis that higher initial microbial load accelerates early biological activity. System A (control) also shows film development, indicating residual microbial populations in the source water. System B shows no decomposition signs yet.
